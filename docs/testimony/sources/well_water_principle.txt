Definition:
The Well Water Principle describes how power, legitimacy, morality, and consciousness continue to flow through a system even after visible institutions lose trust or credibility.
Independence from a system cannot be achieved through distance or rejection alone.
Independence requires rupture at the level of material dependency.
If a group, movement, or individual continues to rely on the outputs of a system, they remain structurally downstream from that system, regardless of stated opposition or distrust.

Core Claim:
You are not independent from a system you are still materially downstream from.
Rejection of authority does not stop power flow.
Distrust of institutions does not sever dependency.
Alternative pipes do not create alternative sources.

The Water Chain Model:
The principle is illustrated through a simple physical chain:
Rain → Soil → Aquifer → Well → Mouth

Power → Institutions → NGOs/Unions → Workers → Consciousness.
