Thesis: 
"Redneck dictatorship" = the class rule of producers expressed in plain country common sense: law-and-order for the crooked (capital), mercy and dignity for the working (labor), and loyalty to the whole (nation) over the vanity of elites. In Marxist terms, that’s the dictatorship of the proletariat translated into a working-class patriotic dialect.

Core Marxist Categories:
- "Earn your keep; no freeloaders" -> Labor produces value; capital appropriates surplus.
- "Keep the peace" -> The state as instrument of class rule (anti-monopoly, public works, price controls).
- "Country first" -> Worker-farmer-soldier coalition; sovereignty over finance capital & offshoring.
- "Don't let suits run our lives" -> Struggle against the PMC (professional-managerial class).
- "We built this place" -> Producerism -> proletarian hegemony.
- "Fair prices, steady work" -> Reproduction of labor power / planned stability.

Historical Lesson: 
Common sense becomes durable only when it captures the commanding heights (finance, energy, logistics) and stabilizes prices, jobs, and dignity.

The American Pathway:
1. Identity as duty.
2. Betrayal by liberal professionalism (Offshoring).
3. Populist reflex (Symbolic solutions).
4. Material turn (Same boss, same bills).
5. Conscious producer politics.

Lexicon Bridge:
- "Dictatorship of the proletariat" -> Producer rule / shop-floor sovereignty.
- "Seize the means" -> Put the shop keys in workers' hands.
- "Expropriate the expropriators" -> No more getting paid for other people's work.
- "Internationalism" -> Fair deals for workers on both sides of borders so bosses can’t play us off each other.
