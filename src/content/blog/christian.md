## The room changed before the sermon did

Many Christians can feel it: the language is still faith, but the operating system feels managerial.

- Metrics replace witness.
- Branding replaces formation.
- Compliance replaces discipleship.

You are not imagining this tension.

## Institutional drift is structural

When churches absorb corporate logic, they optimize for throughput, risk control, and donor confidence.

That produces familiar symptoms:

1. Pastoral care turns into workflow.
2. Leadership becomes image management.
3. Moral language gets used as a retention tool.

The institution survives, but the felt reality of communion thins out.

## Why faithful people feel disoriented

You were formed to recognize covenant, responsibility, and service. When those are repackaged as performance KPIs, your conscience detects contradiction.

That contradiction creates fatigue:

- you keep serving,
- you keep giving,
- but the structure feels hollow.

## A plain diagnosis

This is not "loss of faith" by default. Often it is recognition that administrative logic has overrun spiritual purpose.

The move is not cynicism. The move is clarity:

- separate Gospel language from management language,
- identify where care is real and where it is procedural,
- protect the parts of church life that are still alive.

Faith can remain intact while the institution is honestly diagnosed.
