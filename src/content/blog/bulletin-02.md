# Technical Bulletin 02: The Theology of the Wrench

## The Friction of Sentimental Prayer
Modern faith often dissipates into "sentimental prayer"—a form of ungrounded energy that seeks to change the world through internal emotional shifts. It is like trying to fix a seized engine by hoping it into motion. It ignores the physical resistance of the machine.

## Mechanical Realignment: The Liturgy of Repair
In contrast, we propose the **Theology of the Wrench**. Prayer is not an emotional state; it is the physical act of realignment. When you take a wrench to a loose bolt, you are participating in a liturgy of repair. You are acknowledging the material constraints of the world and applying the necessary torque to restore structural integrity.

Matter precedes hierarchy. If the foundation is out of plumb, no amount of sentimental energy will keep the roof from collapsing. Realignment is mechanical, undeniable, and mandatory.
