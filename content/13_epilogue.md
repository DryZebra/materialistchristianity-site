# **Epilogue: Still Walking** {#epilogue:-still-walking}

You’re reading this epilogue several months after it was written. And I wrote this several months after finishing the book. So in a way, I’m speaking to you from two futures at once, from the perspective of the man who first put these pages together, and from the man who lived through what came after.

After I wrote this, I kept going to church. I kept singing. I kept praying, even if I wasn’t sure to whom. I stood beside people I didn’t fully agree with and loved them anyway. And they loved me. Not because I changed my mind, not because I passed some test of faith, but because I showed up. Because I worked. Because I listened. I’ve taught. I’ve served. I’ve made food for funerals and hugged strangers and watched children be baptized into something I still don’t believe in, but that I respect. Deeply. Fiercely. Reverently.

I’m still an atheist at the time I’m writing this. No angels have visited me. No light has split the sky. I’ve received no secret word from God. But my heart is open. Not to belief, maybe. But to *recognition*. If He is real, I am not hiding. I am not mocking. I am not resisting. I’m just walking the path as honestly as I can, and trusting that whatever matters will remain in motion. I have no pride left. Only a desire to remain good.

So if you’re here expecting a twist, some final moment of salvation, some confession of spiritual awakening, I can’t give you that. What I can give you is something I’ve come to trust more than any miracle: *continuity*. I am still who I was. But I am not where I was. And something about that feels like grace, even if I don’t yet believe in the Giver.

What happens next is not up to me. It never was. This is where I stood. This is what I saw. The rest is in motion.
