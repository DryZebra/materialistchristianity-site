## Forensic Summary

In Materialist Christianity, reality is not a collection of static objects or abstract ideas; it is the sum total of **motion** and its resulting consequences. To say that a thing "exists" is to observe that it moves and that its movement exerts pressure on other things. Static definitions are seen as hollow masks; the only authoritative record of truth is the track left by a motion over time.

If a belief does not result in a change of behavior or a physical consequence, it lacks reality. Existence is therefore a dynamic state of "doing" rather than a passive state of "being."
