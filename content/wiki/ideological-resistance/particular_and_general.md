## Successive Abstraction: The Mechanism of Generalization

If morality is born in the mud of particular suffering, how does a highly specific instance of working-class survival evolve into a general moral command recognized across civilizations? The mechanism for this evolution is **Successive Abstraction**, which can be understood through an analogy to computational linguistic smoothing models (like the **TnT tagger**).

### The TnT Tagger Analogy
In advanced statistical systems, a model cannot rely on a universal assumption for unknown data. Instead:
1. It begins with a **highly specific, localized data point** (the particular suffix of a word).
2. It generates a rigid probability distribution based on how that specific point behaved in the past.
3. It subjects the data to **Successive Abstraction**, systematically stripping away localized characters to find an increasingly more general context until a functional rule emerges.
4. It uses **linear interpolation** to smooth sparse reality into a functional, overarching model.

### The Grinding Wheel of History
This process mirrors human moral development. A localized community faces a particular crisis (famine, Roman occupation, labor exploitation). Through immense suffering and labor, they discover a specific behavioral protocol that allows them to survive. This forms a localized **Sub-Object**.

As history progresses, this protocol is subjected to historical successive abstraction. The hyper-specific details are smoothed out by time, and the core diagnostic is generalized to apply to universal contexts. 

Over millennia, these abstracted survival protocols crystallize into what humanity named **"God"**. In the forensic lens, "God" is not a ghost, but the name for the **generalized, living accumulated consciousness** of billions of people moving toward survival and justice.
