# Feeling the Friction: The Power of the Many

In Materialist Christianity, there is a physical fact about how power works in a community: **Feeling the Friction**. This is a lens for seeing that while one worker standing up for himself is a [Spark](/wiki/node/the_dialectical_leaven), a whole group of workers standing together is a **Power Plant**. This way of thinking helps a person see how individual [Resistance](/wiki/node/antagonistic_labor) turns into a **Collective Momentum** that can stop the [Vampires](/wiki/node/social_vampirism) and fix a town.

### I. The Rhythm of the Job
To understand this lens, you only have to look at a large crew working on a single task—like raising a barn or pulling a heavy cable. If everyone pulls at different times, nothing moves. But if someone starts a "Heave-Ho" rhythm, and everyone pulls together, the weight moves with ease. This isn't just "Cooperation"; it is the discovery of **Synchronized Power**. 

When a person looks through this lens, they see the **Signal**:
- **Common Voice**: When a whole town starts saying the same [Truth](/wiki/node/the_materialist_axiom) at the same time, the noise of the media and the politicians gets drowned out. 
- **Common Action**: When every family in a neighborhood decides to boycott a [Vampire](/wiki/node/social_vampirism) business, the business fails instantly. 

This is what we call [Antagonistic Resonance](/wiki/node/antagonistic_resonance)—the physical "Shaking" of a captured system by the synchronized motion of the folks.

### II. The One Accord
This lens relies on a simple biblical truth that any American worker understands:

- **The Real Shout**: The Bible tells the story of the walls of Jericho falling down because the people all "Shouted with a Great Shout" at once. For a worker, that means that **Shared focus produces a physical result**. It isn't "Magic"; it is the result of the [Momentum](/wiki/node/the_lhc_model) hitting the structure all at once.
- **The Real Unity**: The New Testament describes the first groups of workers as being "Of One Accord." For a worker, that means having **Matched Torque**. It means everyone is putting their shoulder to the same wheel. This lens reveals the [Vampire’s Fear](/wiki/node/redneck_dictatorship)—they are terrified of the day the producers stop fighting each other and start resonating together.

### III. The Outcome: A World that Moves as One
The value of this way of thinking is that it provides a **Tool for Victory**. It shows that you don’t have to "Convince" everyone of every little idea; you just have to find the [One Accord](/wiki/node/the_biblical_baseline) that results in survival. The success of Feeling the Friction is measured by the change in the town. If a parasitic system is dismantled because the folks refused to cooperate, then the lens is verified. The [Church](/wiki/node/the_church_as_structural_anchor) is the place where we find our "Rhythm" and learn to pull together.
