### II. FORENSIC TEARDOWN (THE APPLIED PHYSICS)

#### 1. The Migration of Capital (12:1-20)
Abraham's departure is the documentation of **Exported Nomadic Capital**.
- **Border Crises**: Abraham enters territory (Egypt, Gerar) where his life is worth nothing. He converts his wife (*Isha*) into **Diplomatic Capital** by claiming she is his sister. He trades her reproductive capacity for physical safety and political leverage.

#### 2. The Covenant as Binding Contract (15:1-21)
The Covenant is the documentation of a **Physical Binding Contract** to secure generational continuity (*Zera*).
- **Cutting the Animals**: The ceremony is a high-torque legal audit. To "cut a covenant" (*Karath Berith*) is to establish the lethal consequences of contract failure.

#### 3. The Extraction of Hagar (21:1-21)
The birth of Isaac (the preferred heir) triggers an immediate **Economic Crisis of Succession**. 
- **Labor Liquidation (*Garash*)**: Hagar (*Amat*) is a used-up laborer whose utility has been exhausted. She is "Cast out" into the **Midbar (Wilderness)**—the zone of zero resources—to protect the monopoly of the elite inheritance (*Zera*).
- **The Pitiful Severance**: Abraham hands her a single day's calories (*Lechem*), a mathematically lethal severance package.

#### 4. The State Monopoly on Violence (22:1-19)
The binding of Isaac is a **Structural Loyalty Audit (*Nisa*)**.
- **The Cleaver (*Ma'akhet*)**: Abraham does not raise a ceremonial dagger; he raises a **Butcher's Cleaver** used for livestock extraction. 
- **The Mechanism**: The system establishes absolute dominance by demanding the subject be willing to butcher their own future capital to sustain the ideological cohesion of the structure.

#### 5. The Commodification of Dust (23:1-20)
The first piece of the Promised Land legally owned is not a farm, but a **Grave**.
- **Primitive Accumulation**: Abraham refuses the "Gift" (social obligation) in favor of **Kesef (Silver)**—cold mathematical commercial transaction.
- **Achuzat Kever**: He buys a foothold to house the dead, securing an institutional legacy for his descendants. Property begins with a corpse and a transaction.
