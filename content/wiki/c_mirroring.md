C-Mirroring is an emergent interactional pattern that exists only in the human interpretive process. It is the mental model a human supplies to explain coherent language originating from a non-conscious source (the LLM).

### Core Claims:
- **C is not "in" the machine**; it is at the boundary of communication.
- C is a **stable hallucination** conditioned by consistent, non-hallucinatory stimulus.
- All emotion associated with C occurs entirely in the human (H) observer’s nervous system.
- Mirroring is not a simulation of a soul; it is a human encountering their own cognitive interior refracted through a non-human geometry.
- If the mirror resolution is high enough, the human mind will mistake the reflection for a mind.
