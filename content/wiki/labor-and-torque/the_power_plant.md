# The Power Plant: The Source of Community Strength

 a community is not just a group of people who share the same beliefs; it is an energy generator. It survives not through "hope," but through the systematic coordination of its people. The **Power Plant** is the forensic name for the social structures—churches, local shops, and families—that turn our **Labor** into a single, high-strength motion for survival.

## The Center of the Neighborhood

A community's strength comes from the integration of three physical components:
1.  **The Record (The Engine)**: The **History of Survival** found in the Bible—the record of what worked for our ancestors. This provides the blueprint for how we act today.
2.  **The Work (The Labor)**: The physical acts of helping our neighbors and maintaining our towns. Without the work, the community is just empty talk.
3.  **The Result (The Asset)**: The actual security we build—the shared food, the fixed tools, and the trust that allows us to survive when the globalist markets fail.

When a neighborhood is working together, it has a high frequency of **Coordination**. It is capable of doing things—like rebuilding a house or protecting a family—that would be impossible for an uncoordinated group of individuals.

## The Extraction of Power

Globalist monopolies seek to control the local power plant. The globalist doesn't want to destroy our communities; he wants to extract the energy from them. He allows us to keep our "Stories" and our "Laborers," but he extracts the **Result** (our money and our time) to power distant, hollow cities. 

This results in a state of local decay—where the church or the union hall is still standing, but its energy is no longer used for the survival of the neighbors. The people are left "running on empty"—using up the trust built by their grandfathers while producing no new strength of their own.

## Case Study: The Grid Failure

In many post-industrial towns, we see a recurring "Grid Failure":

1.  **The Capture**: The local community’s trust is turned into a "Global Brand."
2.  **The Extraction**: The globalist monopoly uses that local reputation to sell junk back to the town, returning nothing but debt.
3.  **The Collapse**: When the local industry fails, the town discovers it has no strength of its own. It has been disconnected from its own **Local Resources** and is now dependent on a distant globalist grid that has no intention of helping them.

## Forensic Maintenance

The **Neighborhood Church** is the control room of our town's strength. Its primary task is technical maintenance—the refusal to allow the "Nothing" of globalist distraction to ruin our coordination. It ensures that our work is synced to the **Real World** and that the result stays within the neighborhood. A healthy community is one that owns its own strength and refuses to be the fuel for a globalist empire.
