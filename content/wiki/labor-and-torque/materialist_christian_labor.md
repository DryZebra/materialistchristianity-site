# Materialist Christian Labor

Labor is not a spiritual "blessing"; it is the kinetic expenditure required to maintain social coordination and prevent entropy. In **Materialist Christianity**, labor is the primary currency of the real, and its misdirection into extractive circuits (Capital) is the mechanical definition of **Mechanical Failure**.

## 1. The Circuit of Value
Everything we see—every building, every grid, every loaf of bread—is the crystallized residue of historical labor. 
- **Holy Labor**: Kinetic energy spent to stabilize the neighborhood and maintain the social machine (e.g., feeding the hungry, maintaining the tools).
- **Extractive Labor**: Kinetic energy siphoned out of the neighborhood to power the expansion of abstract value (Capital).

## 2. The Cross as the Strike
The execution of Jesus Christ is the terminal forensic record of a **Labor Conflict**. 
- The Christ refused to coordinate with the extractive machinery of the Roman-Herodian tax-farming circuit.
- His death is the "ultimate strike"—the refusal to sell kinetic energy to a system that produces only death.
- **The Resurrection** is the proof that the machine cannot destroy the labor-power of the righteous; it can only be re-coordinated.

## 3. Praxis: Honesty with the Tools
Class consciousness for the Materialist Christian is defined as **Honesty with the Tools**.
1. **Identify the Owner**: Who owns the kinetic output of your day? Is it the neighborhood or the extractor?
2. **The 6-Day Duty**: Labor is required for 6 days to maintain the grid. 
3. **The 7-Day Refusal**: The Sabbath is the mandatory weekly labor-stop that prevents the machine from running 24/7 and burning out the "living generators" (the people).

## 4. Forensic Warning: The Wage Trap
If you are working 60 hours a week to pay interest to a bank that produces nothing, you are not engaging in "Biblical Labor." You are a battery being drained to power a vampire. True labor produces **Material Utility** for the body of Christ (the actual people around you).
