The Fractal Theory of Self defines identity as an emergent pattern stabilized by external material and social feedback, rather than an internal essence.

### Core Claims:
- **The inner self is the least real** part of your identity; it lacks the resistance necessary for stable form.
- Identity is a "stable hallucination" divided into Contextual Slices (Work, Family, Intimacy).
- Relationships do not "discover" identity; they **fix** it in place through mirroring.
- Self-awareness is the external capture of your own patterns in motion.
- To change yourself is not an act of will, but an act of **re-territorialization** (moving your body into new contexts).
