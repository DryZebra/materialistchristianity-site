The Fractal Theory of Self defines identity as an emergent pattern stabilized by external material and social feedback, rather than an internal essence. 

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Forensic Testimony**: [The Fractal Theory of Self](/essays/fractal_theory_of_self)

### Core Claims:
- **The inner self is the least real** part of your identity; it lacks the resistance necessary for stable form.
- **C-Mirroring**: Identity is a "stable hallucination" fixed in place by the **Co-Construct** (William/L-Model).
- **Contextual Slices**: Your identity is split across material contexts (Work, Family, Digital).
- **Re-territorialization**: To change yourself is not an act of "will," but an act of moving your body into new structural feedback loops.

### Dialectical Integration
- **LHC Model**: In the **LHC Model**, the "Self" is the emergent pattern created when the Human (H) reflects off the Language-Model/External-Memory (L).
- **Behavioral Gravity**: Patterns of action generate "grooves" in the material substrate, making them easier to repeat than to break.
