# The Biblical Baseline: The Metric of Social Stability

In the materialist dialectic, the "Biblical Baseline" is the technical reference point against which all human social motion and moral trajectories must be measured. The Bible is not a collection of "Stories" in the symbolic sense, but a high-density archive of **Sedimented Consequence**. It is the compiled record of how the "Machine" (Humanity) has interacted with the "Real" (Causal Reality) over thousands of years of survival.

## The Archive of the Real

Materialist Christianity views the Bible as a technical manual for the maintenance of high-torque trust and structural durability. It is "Standardized" because historical necessity is standardized.
-   **Protocol Stability**: The patterns found in the baseline (e.g., [[moral_labor]], [[local_vs_public_trust]], Jubilee) are not "Moral Suggestions." They are the protocols that have historically prevented metabolic collapse.
-   **The Rock of Sediment**: The text represents the "Sediment" of thousands of years of human labor. To ignore the baseline is to ignore the cumulative data of human survival.
-   **Resilience to Trends**: While social "Trends" (Liberalism/The Ghost) are fluid and entropic, the baseline is static. It does not move to accommodate the desires of the subject; the subject moves to align with the baseline.

## The Forensic Requirement

To use the biblical baseline is to perform a forensic audit of the present.
1.  **Calibration**: Identifying the core material requirements encoded in the text (e.g., the requirement for [[honesty_with_hardware]]).
2.  **Observation**: Measuring the current displacement of a structure (a church, a family, a union) from that baseline.
