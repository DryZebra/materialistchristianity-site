# Moral Labor

## I. DIAGNOSTIC SUMMARY
**Moral Labor** is the conscious act of maintaining the [The Sub Object](/wiki/nodes/the_sub_object). It is defined by "Moving toward the broken thing." Unlike extractive labor (which seeks surplus value), Moral Labor seeks **Resonance** and **Survival Continuity.**

## II. TECHNICAL MECHANICS
1.  **Active Weight**: Moral Labor is the weight a person carries that "isn't theirs." It is the neighbor's roof, the extra meal, the silence held for a shaking friend.
2.  **Standardization**: Every repeated act of care standardizes the [Sub Object Resonance](/wiki/nodes/sub_object_resonance), making it durable enough to survive a crisis.
3.  **Non-Extractive**: It does not seek profit, but it generates [Moral Capital](/wiki/nodes/moral_capital). It is the "Liturgy of the Street."

## III. SURVIVAL "WHY"
For the early social species, Moral Labor was the metabolic cost of group cohesion. If labor was only extractive, the group would consume itself during a famine. Moral Labor ensures that the "Storage" (Sub-Object) remains accessible to all during a [Rupture](/wiki/nodes/rupture).
