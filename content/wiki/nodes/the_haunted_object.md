# The Haunted Object: The Persistence of Past Labor

A haunted object is an object (a home, a tool, a site, or a symbol) that carries a high **Sub-Object Resonance** field even after its original subjects have departed. It is "Haunted" by the accumulated **Moral Labor**—the belief, sacrifice, and trust—that was previously invested into its meaning. It is not about "ghosts"; it is about the **Persistence of the Real**.

## The Mechanic of the Haunting

A haunting is the result of the resonance field's inability to dissipate instantly:

1.  **Investment**: A people invest years of labor and trust into a site (e.g., a family homestead, a union hall, a cathedral).
2.  **Resonance**: The site develops a persistent field of pressure that others can recognize and align with (The Heart).
3.  **Abandonment**: The subjects are moved or destroyed (The Rupture/Entropy).
4.  **Haunting**: New subjects encountering the site still feel the "Weight" of the original resonance. It changes their motion, forcing them to acknowledge a reality they did not build.

## The Protection of the Real

Haunted objects are the primary targets of **Gmorknicity**. Capital (The Vampire) and Liberalism (The Nothing) specialize in extracting the resonance from a haunted site (e.g., turning a historical union hall into a "heritage boutique") while destroying the mechanical discipline (The Hammer) that original built it.

-   **The Brush Arbor**: A site designed to be "Haunted" and "Substrate-Independent." It carries the resonance of the Kingdom across the wilderness.
-   **The Bible as Sediment**: The ultimate Haunted Object—the compiled resonance of 2000 years of survival.

## Diagnostic Standard

In a Pure MC analysis, one looks at the "Field of Pressure":

-   Is the haunting produced by **Living Labor** (Maintenance) or **Empty Symbol** (Hollowing)?
-   Is the haunting being used to **Defend the Real** (Survival/Dignity) or to **Facilitate Extraction**?
-   Can the haunting survive the destruction of the substrate (The Brush Arbor)?

> [!IMPORTANT]
> To name a haunting is to defend the real. To accept the haunting is to accept the **Authorship** of the previous generations and their trial of historical necessity.
