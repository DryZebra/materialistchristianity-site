# Trust as Commodity: The Universal Form of Relation

Trust is not a sentiment; it is the universal commodity form of human interaction. It is the material value generated by **Moral Labor**—the act of fulfilling commitments, fulfilling needs, and sustaining survival. In a Materialist Christianity context, trust is the primary "Currency" of any stable structure.

## The Extraction of Trust Capital

Trust functions as "Moral Capital." Political and economic systems (Capitalism/Liberalism) specialize in extracting this trust without replenishing it:

1.  **Generation**: Trust is built at the local level through real acts of sacrifice and survival (e.g., neighbors membantu neighbors, soldiers protecting kin, workers in a union).
2.  **Extraction**: A larger institution (a corporation, a political party) adopts the symbols of that trust to gain legitimacy.
3.  **Conversion**: The extracted trust is converted into "Trust Capital," used to move people in directions that do not benefit the original generation site.
4.  **Hollowing**: Once the trust is fully extracted, the structure becomes "Hollow." It maintains the symbol, but the "Fruits" are gone.

## The Trust-Replenishment Cycle

A stable, moral structure is one that replenishment the trust it consumes. It does not extracted "Moral Surplus Value" for private gain; it reinvests trust into the collective survival of the subjects.

-   **The Church**: At its mechanical best, the Church is a trust-replenishment engine. It takes individual labor and reconverts it into communal security, shared memory, and historical weight.
-   **The Union**: A structure designed to prevent the extraction of labor-trust by capital.

## Diagnostic Standard

In a Pure MC analysis, one looks at the "Trust Balance" of a structure:

-   Is trust being generated (through behavior) or merely referenced (through slogans)?
-   Is the trust being extracted by an outside force (Gmorknicity)?
-   What is the "Fruits" of the trust—better survival or more efficient extraction?

-   **Hammer**: The structural discipline (rituals/laws) required to protect trust.
-   **Heart**: The trust field (resonance) that makes the structure worth defending.

> [!CAUTION]
> Trust, like any commodity, can be depleted. Once a structure's trust-balance is zero, it cannot be revived by more "Messaging" or "Belief." It must be ruptured and rebuilt through new, living labor—starting again with small, repeatable acts of consequence.
