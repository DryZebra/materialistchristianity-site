# Honesty with Hardware

In the framework of **Materialist Christianity**, the term "Atheist" is not a badge of rebellion or a declaration of scientific superiority. It is a technical protocol described as **Hardware Honesty**.

### The Baseline Calibration
To measure the world accurately, the observer must first clear the field of all unverified variables. If the universe is a closed system of energy and matter, then any "Ghost" introduced into the equation (a Divine Will, a Supernatural Intervention) acts as a systemic error. 

Atheism, in this sense, is the refusal to account for forces that have no measurable mass or predictable consequence. It is the commitment to the **Hardware**—the cold, industrial reality of the biological machine.

### The Problem of the Ghost
When a system relies on a "Ghost" to function, it becomes fragile. If the Ghost is "debunked" or fails to appear, the social structure built upon it collapses. Materialist Christianity avoids this fragility by starting with the **Atheist Axiom**:
> The system must generate survival torque even if there is no one at the wheel.

### Atheism as Integrity
By adopting the Atheist Axiom, the Materialist Christian ensures that their **Moral Labor** is focused on real-world results (Social Survival, Net Net Structural Integrity) rather than pleasing an imaginary observer. 

It is the practice of standing inside a convincing illusion (The Church) without surrendering your responsibility to the underlying material reality (The Hardware).
