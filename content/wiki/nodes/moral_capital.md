# Moral Capital: The Sustainable Reservoir of Meaning

Moral Capital is the literal accumulation of trust, reputation, and behavioral predictability within a social organism (a family, a church, a union, or a nation). It is the "Wealth of the Real"—the structural mass a people use to stabilize their survival and build a "Rock" against the entropy of history.

## The Reservoir of Meaning: Joy vs. Relief

Moral Capital acts as the **Reservoir of Meaning** for a people. It is built through shared sacrifice and the [Resurrection of Agency](/wiki/nodes/the_kingdom_of_labor).

1.  **Joy (Sustainable Resonance)**: A byproduct of [Structural Dignity](/wiki/nodes/dignity_structural) and labor. It increases the reservoir. It is "Owned Happiness."
2.  **Relief ([Borrowed Happiness](/wiki/nodes/borrowed_happiness))**: A byproduct of the [Gmorknic Shortcut](/wiki/nodes/gmorknicity). It provides a temporary dopamine "hit" by draining the reservoir. It is "Debt-Driven Happiness."

Capital extracts the soul by hollowing out the reservoir and selling the subject "Relief" as a replacement for "Joy." A community that is **Debt-Rich** is almost always **Moral Capital-Poor**.

## Diagnostic Standard

In a Pure Materialist Christianity context, we use the "Inventory of the Heart" to measure the density of a social hedge:

-   **High Density (The Rock)**: High trust, low consumer dependency, shared risk, stable historical memory.
-   **Low Density (The Sand)**: Low trust, high consumer dependency, [Borrowed Happiness](/wiki/nodes/borrowed_happiness) through debt, fragmented identity.
-   **The Debt Check**: Is the community's happiness an asset they built through labor, or a liability they borrowed through a purchase?
-   **The Weight Check**: Does the community have the weight to carry its own members, or must it buy [Social Proximity](/wiki/nodes/social_proximity) from a [Social Vampire](/wiki/nodes/social_vampirism)?

> [!CAUTION]
> Once the Moral Capital of a group is depleted, they become "Dopamine-Poor." At this stage, they are vulnerable to the Vampire, who sells them a simulation of belonging in exchange for their final residues of Labor.
