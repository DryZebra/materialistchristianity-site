# Keeping the Value: Labor that Stays Home

In Materialist Christianity, there is a hard rule about where your energy goes: **Don't Feed the Thieves**. This is a lens for seeing that "Non-Extractive Labor" isn't a political theory; it is **Common Sense Economics**. It means working in a way where the fruits of your labor stay in your own neighborhood and your own family, rather than being sucked away by a big bank or a billionaire in another state. We call this "Keeping the Value," but a worker knows it as **Building Your Own House**.

### I. The Roof Rule
To understand this lens, you only have to look at two different ways to spend a Saturday. In one way, you spend the day helping your neighbor fix his leaking roof. You work hard, but the **Value Stay Home**—your neighbor has a dry house, you have a stronger [Shared Trust](/wiki/node/shared_trust), and the town is one step further from [Rotting](/wiki/node/the_slow_rot_and_the_new_build). That labor is "Non-Extractive."

In the other way, you work a double-shift at a big-box retail store so you can buy a new TV. That labor is **Extractive**. The profit from your work goes to a corporate vault a thousand miles away, and the TV will be broken in five years. Your energy is "Spent" and your town gets nothing. 

We prioritize the **Local Circle**. We follow the "Barn-Raising" model where every hour of work makes the people around us [Denser and Stronger](/wiki/node/dignity_structural).

### II. The Refreshing Yoke
This lens relies on a simple biblical truth that every worker understands:

- **The Mandatory Stop**: The Bible says we must follow the [Sabbath](/wiki/node/the_biblical_baseline)—a day to stop all market-work. For a worker, that is a **Wall against the Vampire**. It is the day when the machine stops being "Spent" by the bosses and starts being "Re-Charged" by the family and the neighbors.
- **The Hands that Give**: The Bible says to work with your hands so "That you may have to give to him that needeth." For a worker, that is **Structural Restitution**. It means the goal of work isn't to "Store up numbers in a bank," but to have the [Real Tools](/wiki/node/the_materialist_axiom) and [Real Food](/wiki/node/moral_communism) to protect the [Kingdom of Labor](/wiki/node/the_kingdom_of_labor).

### III. The Outcome: A World that Wins
The value of this way of thinking is that it provides **Sustainability**. It shows that a town that keeps its own value can never be "Closed down" by a far-away boss. The success of the Keeping-the-Value lens is measured by **Resilience**. If a community has its own [Mutual Aid Fund](/wiki/node/shared_trust), its own skilled men, and its own [Common Pantry](/wiki/node/moral_communism), then the lens is verified. The [Church](/wiki/node/the_church_as_structural_anchor) is the "Local Vault" where we store our labor and keep it safe from the [Vampires](/wiki/node/social_vampirism).
