## Forensic Summary

**Sub-Objects** are the foundational building blocks of social reality. Unlike physical objects (Object) or individual thoughts (Subject), a Sub-Object is a **relational structure** that only exists when behavior is coordinated across multiple people over time. Examples include currency, the law, a church liturgy, or the "Monday-Friday" work week.

A Sub-Object has real mass (**Behavioral Gravity**); if you stop "choosing" to believe in money, the Sub-Object of the Market will still force you into specific survival motions. They are the "instructions" left behind by accumulated labor, and they are the only thing that preserves human coordination across generations.

### 1. The Economic Layer (Sub-Object Materialism)
In its economic form, the sub-object represents the **Abstraction of the Real**. Money and Debt are not just "tools"; they are autonomous structures that command the very hands that built them.
- **The Real**: Human labor time (8 hours of life).
- **The Sub-Object**: A digital entry in a bank ledger (Credit).
- **The Contradiction**: The "Shadow" (credit) eventually begins to command the "Body" (labor), leading to "Blind Contradictions" where finance capital consumes its own productive base.

### 2. The Resonance Layer (Field Effect)
**Sub-Object Resonance** is the field of expectation generated by a structure. When a group collectively moves in alignment with a sub-object (e.g., a ritual or a policy), they generate a resonant field that acts back on individuals.
- **Motion over Belief**: Resonance is generated by repetition of motion, not sincerity of belief.
- **Gravity**: High-resonance fields make compliance feel "light" and resistance feel "heavy."

### 3. The Forensic Alignment (Volume II)
This node is integrated with the Volume II discovery of **The Machine**. 
- The **Sub-Object** is the external architecture; **The Machine** is the internal wiring that responds to it.
- **Well Water Principle**: We are "downstream" from these sub-objects. You cannot achieve independence from a sub-object you are still materially dependent on for survival.
