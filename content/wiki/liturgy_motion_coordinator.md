# Liturgy: The Social Motion Coordinator

Liturgy is not about "worshiping" a spiritual concept; it is the mechanical **Protocol of Synchronization** for a large group of people. In a Materialist Christianity context, Liturgy is the "Software" of the **Social Synchronization Engine**—it provides the shared rhythms, language, and behavioral expectations that allow thousands of disparate subjects to function as a single **Social Organism**.

## The Mechanic of the Sync

Liturgy coordinates motion through the systematic repetition of shared protocols:

1.  **Rhythm**: The shared cycles (The Church Calendar, The Sabbath, The Seasons) that provide a "Common Time" for all subjects.
2.  **Language**: The shared symbols and definitions (The Rock/The Gospel) that allow subjects to trust one another's behavior before they ever meet.
3.  **Alignment**: The physical coordination of thousands of bodies in a single motion (Prayer, Song, Sacraments).
4.  **Fruits**: Increased shared trust, mutual security, and historical durability (Moral Capital).

## The Coordinator vs. The Show

A "Healthy" liturgy is one that facilitates local survival and dignity. A "Captured" liturgy (The Wardrobe/Liberalism) is a "Show"—it uses the same symbols to coordinate **Extraction** (The Vampire). It maintains the "Story" (The Heart) but removes the "Discipline" (The Hammer) that original made the motion consequential.

-   **The Brush Arbor**: A site of **Minimal Liturgy**. When the institution is hollowed, the subjects strip the liturgy down to the "Portable Seed"—the essential protocols of survival.
-   **Hammer**: The structural discipline (rituals/laws) that performs the coordination.
-   **Heart**: The trust field (resonance) of the synchronized people.

## Diagnostic Standard

In a Pure MC analysis, one looks at the "Efficiency" of the coordination:

-   Is the liturgy producing **Survival** (Moral Capital) or into **Waste** (Hollow Ritual)?
