# Local vs. Public Trust: The Scale of Liability

In Materialist Christianity, we distinguish between two fundamentally different types of social coordination: **Local Trust** and **Public Trust**. Local Trust is the forensic residue of direct, high-friction [Moral Labor](/wiki/node/moral_labor) and face-to-face liability within a specific geography or tribe. Public Trust is a "Liberal" and abstract performance—a "Vibe" generated for a collective image or a market brand. We identify Local Trust as the only durable form of [Shared Trust](/wiki/node/shared_trust) capable of withstanding the vacuum of [The Nothing](/wiki/node/the_nothing) and the hollowing of the [Industrial Real](/wiki/node/the_industrial_real).

Public Trust is effectively a [Haunted Object](/wiki/node/the_haunted_object)—a non-functional representation of coordination that lacks the metabolic weight of actual community. It is the syntax used by [Social Vampirism](/wiki/node/social_vampirism) to simulate stability while siphoning [Moral Capital](/wiki/node/moral_capital). Local Trust, by contrast, is a physical asset; it is the "Gearing" that allows a community to move as a single [Machine](/wiki/node/the_machine) because every node possesses a verified [Track Record](/wiki/node/the_materialist_axiom) of liability to its neighbors.

### Dynamics of Trust Scales
- **The Neighbor vs. The World (Luke 10:25-37)**: The parable of the Good Samaritan is a forensic audit of **Local Trust**. The "Neighbor" is defined not by legal status or "Public" sentiment, but by the physical act of mercy and metabolic support (Moral Labor). The Priest and the Levite represent "Public Trust"—the hollowed-out institutions that perform the ritual but refuse the liability. The Samaritan's trust is "Local" because it is grounded in the material requirement of the immediate coordinate crisis.
- **The Friction of Proximity**: Local Trust is "Warm" and high-friction; it requires the constant expenditure of metabolic energy to maintain. We treat this friction as a diagnostic for truth. Any system that claims to offer "Trust" without the friction of personal liability (e.g., automated market trust, liberal philanthropy) is forensically identified as a vehicle for [Structural Betrayal](/wiki/node/the_dialectic_betrayal). True stability is built only at the scale of the "Hand-to-Hand."
- **The Reconstruction of the Table**: The materialist mission is to dismantle the dependency on "Public" simulations and rebuild the infrastructure of **Local Trust**. This is the process of building the [Kingdom of Labor](/wiki/node/the_kingdom_of_labor) from the ground up, starting with the [Sovereign Table](/wiki/node/rituals_of_consequence). By re-establishing local liability, we create a social structure that possesses enough [Structural Dignity](/wiki/node/dignity_structural) to resist the entropic hollowing of the state and the market.
