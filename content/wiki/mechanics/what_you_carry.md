# What You Carry

In a 15-man shop, everyone knows who's carrying the weight. If a truck needs to be loaded by 5 PM, and three guys are slacking, the other twelve feel the **Pressure**. This isn't just about physical physics; it's the **Moral Weight** of the work.

## The Productive Burden
If you work for a living, you are constantly carrying something for someone else. You carry the safety of the driver who takes your gear; you carry the trust of the customer who uses your tool; you carry the livelihood of the small business owner who has to make payroll. This is **Productive Labor**. It is the "Real Work" that reproduces social life. 

## The Vampiric Detachment
The **Extractors** (finance, monopoly managers, globalists) don't carry anything. They have "Equivalent Price" instead. If a project fails, they just move their money to another one. They don't feel the weight of the delay or the cost of the mistake. They are detached from the **Flesh** of the work.

- **Mammon's Way**: You carry nothing but your own "Worth." Everything has an equivalent price.
- **God's Way**: You carry your brother's burden (Galatians 6:2). The work is yours, and there is no price for it.

## The Real Split in the Shop
The "Everyone Else" are the ones who manage the work without ever touching the weight. They are the paper-shufflers and the "vampires" who skim the value off your back without ever feeling the pressure of the deadline or the fear of a broken tool.

## Summary
"What You Carry" is the proof of your material reality. If you feel the pressure of the work on your back, you are in the **Working Class**. If you are serving the **Rival Master**, you are the one adding more weight to others' backs while carrying none yourself.
