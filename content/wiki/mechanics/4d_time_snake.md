# Planning for the Future: The Long View

In Materialist Christianity, **Planning for the Future** is the physical ability to see past the immediate crisis or the next paycheck. Instead of living merely in the "now" (which is the goal of consumer debt and advertising), the worker must build infrastructure that lasts for their children.

## Moving Past the Immediate

Globalist extraction relies on keeping people trapped in a 24-hour cycle of panic, outrage, or consumption. When you are only thinking about what you will buy or watch tomorrow, you cannot build a town.

1.  **The Generational View**: A strong neighborhood thinks in terms of decades. When they dig a well or build a shop, they are not just solving a problem for today; they are creating a physical asset that their grandchildren will inherit. This is how real "time travel" works—you send strength into the future by building it today.
2.  **Escaping the Debt Trap**: Debt is the primary weapon used to steal the future. By owing the bank for your existence today, you guarantee that your future labor is already owned by someone else. Escaping debt is the prerequisite for owning your own future.
3.  **The Biblical Anchor**: The entire structure of the Old Testament is built around land inheritance and generational continuity. The highest blessing was to see your children's children walk in the same sturdy habits of survival.

## Conclusion

To be a Materialist Christian is to refuse the short-term panic of the current culture. We anchor ourselves in the work that takes years to finish, knowing that true survival is a multi-generational project.
