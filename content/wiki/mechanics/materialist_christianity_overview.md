# Materialist Christianity: A Technical Overview

**Materialist Christianity** is the forensic recovery of the physical and social infrastructure of the Christian faith. It rejects the modern reduction of Christianity to "private belief" or "abstract idealism." Instead, it analyzes the faith as a materialist engine for the survival and reproduction of the productive class.

## The Forensic Split: Flesh vs. Ghost
The project identifies a fundamental mechanical split in how the world is structured:
1.  **The Flesh (The Real)**: This is the physical reality of the job site, the 15-man shop, the local neighborhood infrastructure, and the **Living Labor** of those who work for a living.
2.  **The Ghost (The Extractor)**: This is the abstract layer of global finance, managerialism, and **Mammon**. It creates "Ghost-Structures" that extract the life from the flesh without providing survival in return.

## The Marxist Diagnostic: Marxism in the Text
Materialist Christianity does not "import" Marxism as an outside ideology; it recognizes Marxist mechanics as the only professional way to describe the hollowing out of the nation.
- **Extraction**: The technical process by which **Mammon** (the system of equivalence) skims the value from the workers and the allied shop owners.
- **Equivalence**: The attempt to turn everything (labor, love, faith) into a price-point.
- **Productive vs. Non-Productive**: The forensic distinction between labor that reproduces social life and "vampiric" labor that merely moves value around.

## The Scriptural Anchor: The Incarnation
The materialist core of Christianity is the **Incarnation**—the Word becoming Flesh. 
- If the faith is not material, it is not Christian. 
- To serve Christ is to serve the material needs of the neighbor (Matthew 25). 
- To serve **Mammon** (Matthew 6:24) is to serve the abstract "Ghost" of the extractor.

## Summary
Materialist Christianity is the **Analysis of the Real**. It provides the tools for the productive class to identify their own value, separate from the extractive cycle of globalist finance, and rebuild the [[the_brush_arbor]]—the local, resilient infrastructure of the Kingdom.
