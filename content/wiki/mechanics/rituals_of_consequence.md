# Work That Matters

In Materialist Christianity, **Work That Matters** (previously called Rituals of Consequence) is any social habit or routine that results in a real, physical outcome for the community. It rejects 'Hollow Rituals'—actions performed for symbolic display or emotional gratification—in favor of synchronized habits that build structural dignity and protect the neighborhood.

## The Diagnostic Tool: Real Habit vs. Symbolic Display

A group of people requires conscious discipline—the synchronization of collective habits toward a verified result. A gathering (like a Sunday church service or a town hall) is audited by its output. If a gathering does not result in the community becoming more skilled, better protected, or more resilient, it is diagnostically categorized as a dead end.

- **The Practice**: The use of collective habits to train for industrial and social survival.
- **The Audit**: Continuously checking every gathering against its physical results. Are the people actually equipped to handle a crisis now?
- **The Discipline**: Synchronizing the folks so that everyone pulls their weight and coordinates without friction.

## Historical Mapping: Decorum and Order

- **1 Corinthians 14:40**: "Let all things be done decently and in order."
- **Materialist Interpretation**: The Biblical archive identifies 'Rituals' not as magical ceremonies, but as proper order. Doing things in 'Order' is the only way to ensure the energy of the community actually fixes the roof, builds the well, or defends the street without people getting hurt or wasting time. 

## The Conclusion

We reject the 'Spectacle' model of modern religion in favor of technical training. Success is verified by the sovereignty of the folks. If a community follows its manual and achieves a state where they are no longer dependent on 'Big Boss' oversight from corporate monopolies, the work was real. The Church acts as the 'Training Hall' where these habits are practiced and perfected.
