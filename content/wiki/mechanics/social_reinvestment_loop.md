# Social Reinvestment Loop: The Replenishment of the Real

A Social Reinvestment Loop is the mechanical process by which a social structure reinvests its accumulated **Moral Capital** (trust, labor, memory) back into its own "Soil"—the subjects who built it. In the real world, this is represented by the **Jubilee Cycle** and the **Tithes of the Church**—mechanisms designed to prevent the extraction of moral labor by an outside "Vampire."

## The Mechanic of the Loop

Reinvestment is not an act of "charity"; it is a structural requirement for stability:

1.  **Accumulation**: The structure gathers moral labor (trust/repetition) into a shared repository (The Church, The Union, The Community Chest).
2.  **Distribution**: The structure identifies points of "Drought" or "Entropy" within the social organism (the poor, the widow, the orphan, the unemployed).
3.  **Replenishment**: The structure invest its trust capital (mutual aid, resource sharing, communal defense) into the "Drought" sites.
4.  **Fruits**: The material consequence of the loop—increased stability, a more durable "Hedge," and the prevention of **Gmorknic Extraction**.

## The Loop vs. The Sucking

A "Healthy" loop is one that maintains its own mass and mass within the community. A "Captured" loop (Capital/Extraction) breaks the circle and converts the reinvestment into "Profit"—extracting it for private consumption and leaving the subjects in entropy.

-   **The Jubilee**: The ultimate reinvestment loop, designed to reset the "Debt" of the subjects and return them to their original "Rock."
-   **The Church Pantry**: A local, repeatable loop that stabilizes survival by re-aligning resources with the **Needs of Labor**.

## Diagnostic Standard

In a Pure MC analysis, one looks at the "Direction of the Loop":

-   Is the trust capital returning to the subjects (Reinvestment) or leaving the site (Extraction)?
-   Is the reinvestment producing **Survival** (Moral Capital) or into **Waste** (Hollow Ritual)?
-   Can the loop survive the destruction of the substrate (The Brush Arbor)?

-   **Hammer**: The structural discipline (rituals/laws) that enforces the reinvestment.
-   **Heart**: The trust field (resonance) generated by the shared security.

> [!CAUTION]
> A reinvestment loop that fails is the first sign of structural "Blight." Once the Vampire enters the loop, the "Soil" begins to die. To defend the Kingdom is to defend the Reinvestment Loop.
