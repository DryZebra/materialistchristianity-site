# Social Vampirism: Identifying Common Theft

 **Social Vampirism** is the name we give to systems that steal the resources of a community. It describes any social or economic group that takes the **Trust** and the **Labor-Time** of its neighbors without putting anything back into the neighborhood’s survival. The "Vampire" (usually a globalist monopoly or a predatory government) stays strong by hollowing out the towns it occupies.

## How Vampirism Works

Vampirism works by breaking the local loop of work and reward:
1.  **Setting the Trap**: The vampire finds a productive town or a strong family and attaches a way to take their money (like a high-interest loan or a globalist store).
2.  **Taking the Strength**: The vampire takes the **Trust** and the hard work of the people. It uses this energy to fuel its own profits and data while leaving the local neighbors in poverty.
3.  **The Final Decay**: Because the vampire doesn't help maintain the neighborhood's tools, houses, or trust, the town eventually falls apart. The vampire then moves on to a new town.

## Biblical Diagnosis: The Wicked Tenants

The technical nature of vampirism is documented in the Parable of the Tenants (Matthew 21:33-46).

-   **The Greedy Managers**: The tenants manage a vineyard (the local business or community) but refuse to give any of the fruit back to the owner.
-   **Refusing to Reinvest**: When messengers are sent to collect the fruit, the tenants beat and kill them. They want to keep all the wealth for themselves instead of helping the land stay healthy.
-   **The Failure of the System**: Jesus identifies this as the rejection of the "Cornerstone." By refusing to put resources back into the neighborhood, the tenants ensure their own destruction when the real world eventually catches up to them.

## Case Study: The Hollowed-Out Town

We see social vampirism in our own history:
-   **The Company Town**: Think of a town where a single giant corporation took the labor of three generations and then abandoned the town the moment it wasn't profitable anymore. The corporation is the vampire; the hollowed-out town is the proof of the theft.
-   **The Digital Vampire**: Think of social media platforms that take your attention and your community’s trust just to sell ads. They give you "likes" (empty talk) while they take the real energy of your relationships.
