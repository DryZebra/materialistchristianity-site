# Social Vampirism: The Diagnostic of Extraction

In the materialist dialectic, social vampirism is the forensic diagnostic of metabolic theft. It describes any social or economic structure that consumes the [[moral_capital]] and labor-time of its subjects without equivalent reinvestment in the group's material survival. The "Vampire" (often Capital or a predatory state) maintains its own entropic growth by hollowing out the "Body" of the community it occupies.

## Structural Mechanics

Vampirism operates through the systematic disruption of the metabolic loop:
1.  **The Capture of Net Net**: The vampire identifies a site of high-torque production (a union, a family, or an industry) and attaches an extractive interface.
2.  **The Extraction of Blood**: "Blood" in this context is the [[moral_capital]] and shared trust of the subjects. The vampire consumes this energy to fuel its own abstract metrics (profit, data, or symbolic power) while the local subjects are left in a state of metabolic poverty.
3.  **The Entropic Result**: Because the vampire does not reinvest in the "Hardware" of the community, the group eventually enters a state of [[rupture]]. The vampire then moves on to a new "Host."

## Biblical Diagnosis: The Wicked Tenants

The forensic nature of vampirism is documented in the Parable of the Tenants (Matthew 21:33-46).

-   **The Extractive Tenants**: The tenants manage a vineyard (the social machine) but refuse to return the fruit to the householder (the source of the machine's instructions).
-   **The Murderous Extraction**: When the householder sends messengers to collect the fruit, the tenants beat and kill them. This is the mechanical refusal to reinvest—the tenants want to "Consume the Inheritance" (the capital) for themselves.
-   **The Forensic**: Jesus identifies this as the rejection of the "Cornerstone." By rejecting the technical requirement for reinvestment, the tenants ensure their own eventual destruction when the Machine (the Householder) returns to re-establish the Real.

## Forensic Observations

Synthesized archival snapshots of social vampirism:
-   **The Company Town**: Observations of post-industrial towns where a single corporation extracted the labor of three generations and then abandoned the town when the substrate became unprofitable. The corporation is the vampire; the hollowed-out town is the forensic evidence of its metabolic theft.
-   **The Digital Vampire**: Observations of "Public Trust" being publicized into digital platforms. The platform extracts the behavioral resonance of the subjects to sell ads while returning only "Glitter" (symbolic dopamine) to the users. The users become entropic and uncoordinated, while the platform achieves god-like torque.
