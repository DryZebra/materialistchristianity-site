# Resistance: Refusing to be Hollowed Out

In Materialist Christianity, **Resistance** is the technical act of refusing to apply your labor to a system that no longer provides survival. It is the forensic diagnostic of the moment the productive class recognizes a rule as **Pointless or Extractive**.

## Forensic Analysis: Stopping the Extraction
In a real job-site, resistance occurs when the official rules of the boss or the globalist monopoly split from the physical reality of the work.
1.  **Refusal of Pointless Rules**: When a globalist extractor demands a motion (like excessive paperwork or administrative safety theatre) that has nothing to do with the actual job or the safety of the neighbor, the productive class recognizes this as a hollow ritual. The refusal to participate is the 
   **Preservation of Real Labor**.
2.  **Stopping for Clarity**: This is not "laziness." It is the technical choice to stop feeding an extractor that is already hollowing out your town. By stopping, you pull your **Work (The Hand)** and your **Attention (The Forehead)** away from the extractor's machine and return them to your neighborhood.
3.  **Local Reinvestment**: Real resistance is not just "saying no"; it is taking that same energy and applying it to a **Local Need**. It is fixing a neighbors truck instead of filling out a globalist compliance form.

## Biblical Diagnosis: The Sabbath Conflict (Matthew 12)
Jesus and his disciples practiced this technical resistance.
- The Pharisees had turned "The Sabbath" into an extractive rule that prevented people from eating (Matthew 12:1-8).
- Jesus' diagnostic—"The Son of Man is Lord even of the Sabbath"—is the materialist proof that **Life and Hunger** are more real than any extractive rule. If the rule doesn't feed the man, the rule is a ghost.

## Summary
**Resistance** is the moment the productive class recognizes a system has stopped providing value. To be a Materialist Christian is to refuse to be the fuel for a globalist extractor that has already retreated from reality.
