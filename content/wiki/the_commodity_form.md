# The Price Tag: Everything for Sale

In Materialist Christianity, there is a name for the state where a community has been captured by [The Money Vampires](/wiki/node/social_vampirism): **The Price Tag**. This is a lens for seeing that some things—like [Shared Trust](/wiki/node/shared_trust), [Labor](/wiki/node/moral_labor), and even your [Home](/wiki/node/local_vs_public_trust)—should never be treated like something you just buy and sell for a profit. We call this "The Commodity Form," but a worker knows it as **Putting a Price on the Sacred**.

### I. The House vs. The Market Rule
To understand this lens, you only have to look at the difference between a **Home** and a **House for Sale**. A "Home" is where you live, where you gather your family, and where you store your memories and your [Tools](/wiki/node/moral_labor). It has "Residency." But when a [Vampire](/wiki/node/social_vampirism) looks at that same building, they don't see a home—they see a piece of "Real Estate." They see a "Commodity" that can be flipped for a profit.

When you treat your community like a **Market**, you lose its [Stability](/wiki/node/the_materialist_axiom). Trust is replaced by "Transactions," and neighbors are replaced by "Passengers" who are only there for as long as it's profitable. Once you put a "Price Tag" on everything, the town loses its [Weight](/wiki/node/dignity_structural) and the [Nothing](/wiki/node/the_nothing) starts to grow.

### II. The House of Merchandise
This lens relies on a simple biblical truth that every worker understands:

- **The Father's House**: The Bible says to not make the "Father's House" a "House of Merchandise." For a worker, that is **Structural Purification**. It’s the act of protecting the community’s [Core Resources](/wiki/node/moral_communism) from being sold off to the highest bidder.
- **The Whip of Small Cords**: When Jesus saw the money-changers in the temple, he didn't "Debate" them—he used physical force to drive them out. He understood that **Extraction** is a [Physical Danger](/wiki/node/behavioral_gravity) that has to be removed to protect the [Kingdom of Labor](/wiki/node/the_kingdom_of_labor).

### III. The Outcome: A World with Value that Stays
The value of this way of thinking is that it helps you **Hold onto What Matters**. It stops you from thinking that "More Money" is always the answer. The success of the Price-Tag lens is measured by **Retention**. If a town can keep its [Tools](/wiki/node/moral_labor) and its [History](/wiki/node/the_bible_as_sediment) for itself, even when the rest of the world is selling everything, then the lens is verified. The [Church](/wiki/node/the_church_as_structural_anchor) is the "Sovereign Zone" where the price tags are removed and the [Shared Trust](/wiki/node/shared_trust) is kept for the people.
